What is Normalization?

Normalization is the process of organizing data in a database to minimize redundancy and dependency. It involves dividing large tables into smaller, related tables and defining relationships between them.

Goal: Eliminate redundant data, ensure data dependencies make sense, and simplify data management.

Normal Forms (NF)

Normal forms are a series of guidelines that help organize your database structure. Each form builds upon the previous one.

1NF - First Normal Form

The most fundamental level of normalization. A table is in 1NF if:

  • Each cell contains a single, atomic value (no repeating groups)
  • Each column contains values of the same data type
  • Each column has a unique name
  • The order of rows and columns doesn't matter
-- VIOLATION of 1NF: Multiple values in one cell
-- Table: customers
customer_id | customer_name | phone_numbers
1           | John Smith    | 555-1234, 555-5678

-- CORRECT: 1NF compliant
customer_id | customer_name | phone_number
1           | John Smith    | 555-1234
1           | John Smith    | 555-5678

2NF - Second Normal Form

A table is in 2NF if it's in 1NF AND has no partial dependencies:

  • Must be in 1NF first
  • No column depends on only part of a composite primary key
  • All non-key columns must depend on the entire primary key
-- VIOLATION of 2NF: Partial dependency
-- Table: order_items (composite key: order_id, product_id)
order_id | product_id | product_name | quantity | price
1        | 100        | Widget       | 2        | 9.99

-- product_name depends only on product_id, not order_id!

-- CORRECT: Split into two tables
-- Table: order_items
order_id | product_id | quantity
1        | 100       | 2

-- Table: products
product_id | product_name | price
100        | Widget       | 9.99

3NF - Third Normal Form

A table is in 3NF if it's in 2NF AND has no transitive dependencies:

  • Must be in 2NF first
  • No column depends on another non-key column
  • Non-key columns don't depend on other non-key columns
-- VIOLATION of 3NF: Transitive dependency
-- Table: employees
emp_id | name   | department_id | department_name | department_location
1      | John   | 10            | Sales          | New York

-- department_name depends on department_id, which depends on emp_id
-- This creates redundancy and update anomalies

-- CORRECT: Split into two tables
-- Table: employees
emp_id | name   | department_id
1      | John   | 10

-- Table: departments
department_id | department_name | department_location
10            | Sales          | New York

Higher Normal Forms (BCNF, 4NF, 5NF)

Beyond 3NF

  • BCNF (Boyce-Codd NF) - Stricter version of 3NF. Every determinant must be a candidate key.
  • 4NF - No multi-valued dependencies. Each record has independent many-to-many relationships.
  • 5NF - Join dependency cannot be decomposed further. Complex scenarios with multiple overlapping join constraints.

Balance: While normalization reduces redundancy, over-normalization can hurt performance. In read-heavy applications (data warehouses, reporting databases), some denormalization may be beneficial for speed.

Denormalization

Denormalization is the intentional process of adding redundant data to improve read performance. It's common in:

  • Data warehouses - For reporting and analytics
  • Caching layers - Redis, Memcached
  • Read replicas - Pre-computed aggregates
  • Star schemas - Fact and dimension tables

Normalization vs Denormalization

Normalized Denormalized

Better for write operations Better for read operations
